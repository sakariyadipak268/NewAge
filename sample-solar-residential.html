So what is Solar PV?

Solar Photovoltaic (PV) allows you to generate cheap, green electricity in your own home by capturing the sun's powerful energy using photovoltaic cells (PV). The great thing is, these solar cells don't even need direct sunlight to work (which is great to learn in the UK as we’re famous for it not ‘always’ being quite as sunny as we’d like!) - but rest assured they can still generate a useful amount of electricity on a dull and cloudy day. The cells convert the sunlight into electricity, which can then be used to power household appliances and lighting.

These very clever PV cells are made from layers of semi-conducting material, usually silicon. When light shines on the cell, it creates an electric field across the layers. The stronger the sunshine, the more electricity is produced. Groups of cells are mounted together in panels that we can either install on your roof or on the ground.

The power of a PV cell is measured in kilowatts peak (kWp). That's the rate at which it generates energy at peak performance in full direct sunlight during the summer. PV cells come in a variety of shapes and sizes. Most PV systems are made up of panels that fit on top of an existing roof or open ground areas, but you can also fit solar tiles too!

Solar Made Simple.

In a nutshell it wortks in your home like this:

Throughout the day, the Solar panels we expertly install on your roof, turns all of that captured sunlight into DC electricity which we capture.

The top grade inverter we install in your home then converts the DC electricity into AC electricity for use throughout your home.

The AC electricity your Solar panels produce, then powers your home appliances instead of using energy from the national grid.

And the best part... any excess energy you generate will either be sent back to the grid or can be stored in batteries for night time use!
